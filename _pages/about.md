Hi there!

I am a molecular and computational biologist with a broad interest in understanding genetic change over time. 

My research is focused on:
* building high-quality genomic resources to understand the evolutionary history between organisms;
* defining genetic relationships within species at both local and global scales; and
* understanding genome-wide variation to map genes associated with traits of interest.

I have worked on a range of different organisms, however, most of my research has focused on nematodes of human and veterinary importance. My main role, currently as part of the BBSRC sLoLa-funded [BUG Consortium](https://bugconsortium.wordpress.com/), is to develop genetic resources to understand anthelmintic resistance in two economically important pathogens of small ruminants, *Haemonchus contortus* and *Teladorsagia circumcincta*.

I am currently a senior Postdoctoral Fellow in the Parasite Genomics group at the Wellcome Sanger Institute near Cambridge, UK. However, I am originally from Melbourne, Australia, and moved to the UK with my wife and son in 2015. Since then, we have added twin boys to the mix, and making the most of UK life and [travels both local and abroad](https://adashofsaffron.wordpress.com/).       

This is my website on worms, genomics, and other stuff I am working on. 

Cheers, 
Stephen
